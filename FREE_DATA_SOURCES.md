# Free Data Sources for LNG Shipments

## Government & Official Sources

### 1. LNG Canada Official Reports
- **Website**: https://www.lngcanada.ca
- **What**: Press releases, quarterly reports, export announcements
- **How**: Check their news section for shipment announcements
- Usually announces first cargoes, milestones

### 2. Canada Energy Regulator (CER)
- **Website**: https://www.cer-rec.gc.ca
- **What**: Export data, licensing information
- **Data**: Monthly/quarterly LNG export volumes
- May have shipment counts and destinations

### 3. Port of Kitimat / Prince Rupert Port Authority
- **Website**: https://www.rupertport.com
- **What**: Vessel schedules, port calls
- **Access**: Some data may be publicly available or requestable

### 4. Natural Resources Canada
- **What**: Energy statistics, export data
- **How**: NRCan publications and data tables

## Free Marine Tracking APIs

### 5. VesselFinder API (Free Tier)
- **Website**: https://www.vesselfinder.com/api
- **Free tier**: 20 requests/day
- **Data**: Basic vessel positions
- **Limitation**: Current data only, no historical

### 6. AISHub (Already integrated)
- **Free access** with registration
- **Better than**: MarineTraffic for cost
- **Limitation**: Delay in data, less detail

### 7. BarentsWatch (Norway)
- **Free API**: https://www.barentswatch.no
- **Data**: AIS data for ships near Norway
- **Use**: If vessels pass through European routes

## Web Scraping Options (Manual)

### 8. ShipSpotting Communities
- **Kitimat Ship Spotters**: Local Facebook/Reddit groups
- **Marine Traffic (free website)**: View ship history manually
- **FleetMon**: Free basic tracking

### 9. LNG Industry Publications
- **LNG Journal**: Industry news
- **Riviera Maritime News**: Shipping announcements
- **TradeWinds**: LNG carrier movements
- Usually report major cargoes and first shipments

### 10. LinkedIn/Twitter
- LNG Canada official accounts often announce shipments
- Vessel operators post about deliveries
- Industry professionals share news
